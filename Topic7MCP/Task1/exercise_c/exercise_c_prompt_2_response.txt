The paper "Attention is All You Need" by Ashish Vaswani, Noam Shazeer, Niki Parmar, Jakob Uszkoreit, Llion Jones, Aidan N. Gomez, Lukasz Kaiser, and I. Polosukhin was published in 2017 at the Neural Information Processing Systems conference. This groundbreaking work introduced the **Transformer architecture**, which relies exclusively on attention mechanisms—eliminating recurrence and convolutions entirely. 

Key achievements include:
- Achieving **28.4 BLEU** on the WMT 2014 English-to-German translation task (surpassing previous best results by over 2 BLEU).
- Setting a new single-model state-of-the-art **41.8 BLEU** on English-to-French translation after just **3.5 days of training on eight GPUs**, significantly reducing training costs compared to prior models.
- Demonstrating strong generalization to other tasks, such as English constituency parsing.

The paper is openly accessible on arXiv: [https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762). This work has since become foundational to modern large language models and sequence-to-sequence tasks.
