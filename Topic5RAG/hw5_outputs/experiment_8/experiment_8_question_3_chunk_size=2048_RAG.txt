People have the right to privacy and protection of personal data, as outlined in the Union's data protection laws. These rights include:

1. Data Minimization - Processing of personal data should be limited to what is strictly necessary for the intended purposes.
2. Data Protection by Design and by Default - Personal data processing is governed by these principles, which require organizations to consider data protection measures from the outset and by default.
3. Comprehensible Information - Providers should provide understandable information on how high-risk AI systems were developed and how they operate over their lifetimes.
4. Special Categories of Personal Data - High-risk AI systems may process special categories of personal data if it is strictly necessary for bias detection and correction, subject to safeguards for fundamental rights and freedoms.
5. Traceability - Keeping records and providing technical documentation is essential for enabling traceability of AI systems, verifying compliance with regulations, and facilitating post-market monitoring.
6. Transparency - AI systems should be designed and used in ways that allow appropriate traceability, explainability, and human control and oversight.
7. Human-Centric Approach - AI should serve people, respect human dignity, and promote fair treatment, avoiding discriminatory impacts and unfair biases.
8. Legal Framework - The principles of privacy, data protection, and accountability are integral to the development and use of AI systems, as stated in the context. 

These rights aim to ensure that AI systems are developed and used responsibly, respecting individuals' privacy and data protection needs.
