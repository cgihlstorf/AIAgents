The context emphasizes several key aspects related to AI data privacy rights:

1. **Privacy Preservation**: Data should be processed in a way that respects privacy, ensuring that personal data are protected throughout the lifecycle of the AI system.

2. **Data Minimization**: Personal data should be collected only as much as necessary, adhering to the principle of data minimization.

3. **Data Protection by Design and Default**: These principles apply to all data processing activities, including AI systems, emphasizing the importance of protecting personal data from inception.

4. **Compliance with Legal Frameworks**: The fundamental right to the protection of personal data is safeguarded under Union data protection laws, specifically through Regulations (EU) 2016/679, (EU) 2018/1725, and Directive (EU) 2016/680.

5. **Access to High-Quality Data**: Access to high-quality data for training, validating, and testing AI systems should be ensured, particularly in sectors like healthcare where it's crucial for non-discriminatory access to health data.

These points collectively highlight the importance of maintaining privacy and security in AI systems, aligning with legal frameworks and ensuring fair access to essential services and benefits.
