The EU AI Act aims to establish harmonized rules on artificial intelligence across the European Union, including provisions on the placement of AI systems on the market, their use, and the protection of consumer rights through representative actions. It also includes measures related to transparency, market monitoring, and compliance deadlines for high-risk AI systems and general-purpose AI models. Additionally, it addresses prohibitions of certain AI practices and requires operators of high-risk AI systems to meet specified requirements. The act seeks to ensure the responsible development and deployment of AI technologies within the EU while protecting consumer interests and ensuring fair competition.
