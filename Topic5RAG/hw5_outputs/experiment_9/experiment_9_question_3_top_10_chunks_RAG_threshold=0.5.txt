People have the right to privacy and to protect their personal data throughout the lifecycle of the AI system. The principles of data minimization and data protection by design and by default, as outlined in Union data protection law, are applicable when personal data are processed. Additionally, the fundamental right to the protection of personal data is safeguarded by Regulations (EU) 2016/679 and (EU) 2018/1725, and Directive (EU) 2016/680. These regulations ensure that personal data is protected during the lifecycle of the AI system. Furthermore, the regulation does not affect existing laws like Regulation (EU) 2016/679 or (EU) 2018/1725, or Directive 2002/58/EC or (EU) 2016/680, without prejudice to Article 10(5) and Article 59 of this Regulation. Lastly, harmonized rules for the placement on the market, putting into service, and use of AI systems should facilitate the exercise of data subjects' rights and other remedies guaranteed under Union law on the protection of personal data and other fundamental rights.
