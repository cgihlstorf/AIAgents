AI systems can provide key competitive advantages to undertakings and support socially and environmentally beneficial outcomes, such as improvements in healthcare, agriculture, food safety, education and training, media, sports, culture, infrastructure management, energy, transport and logistics, public services, security, justice, and resource and energy efficiency. Additionally, they can enhance monitoring and personalisation of services, leading to better participation in society and improved standards of living. Furthermore, while there are concerns about potential negative impacts like power imbalances and adverse effects on fundamental rights, these should be considered alongside the numerous positive contributions AI makes to various sectors and aspects of life. The text emphasizes the importance of addressing these issues through regulation and ethical guidelines to ensure balanced development.
