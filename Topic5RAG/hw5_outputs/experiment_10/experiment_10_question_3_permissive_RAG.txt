The right to privacy and to protection of personal data must be guaranteed throughout the entire lifecycle of the AI 
system. In this regard, the principles of data minimisation and data protection by design and by default, as set out in 
Union data protection law, are applicable when personal data are processed.

People have the right to privacy and protection of personal data throughout the lifecycle of AI systems, as outlined in relevant EU legislation like GDPR and DPIA. This includes ensuring that personal data is handled securely, transparently, and in compliance with data protection laws. The principle of data minimization ensures that only necessary data is collected, while data protection by design and default emphasizes incorporating privacy considerations from the outset. These measures aim to protect individuals' fundamental rights to privacy and data protection. Additionally, the regulation should not affect the fundamental right to the protection of personal data, as it is specifically protected under EU regulations on data protection.
