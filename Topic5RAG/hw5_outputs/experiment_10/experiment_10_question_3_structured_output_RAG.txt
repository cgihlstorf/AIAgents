People have several data privacy rights with respect to AI, including:

- The right to privacy and protection of personal data must be guaranteed throughout the entire lifecycle of the AI system.
- Data should be collected only if it is essential for achieving the purpose of processing, and no more than necessary.
- Personal data should be protected by using technical measures that ensure confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of processing activities.
- Individuals have the right to request rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, objection to processing, portability of their personal data, and object to automated decision-making and profiling based on their data. - They can receive information about how their data is being used, and request transparency regarding the purposes and means of processing. - People have the right to contest decisions made by AI systems that significantly affect them, ensuring fairness and accountability. - Their fundamental rights to data protection and privacy must be safeguarded, particularly through regulations like GDPR and DPIA. - Access to data should be provided in a timely manner, and institutions providing or supporting access to data should contribute to high-quality data for AI training, validation, and testing. - Sectoral authorities, including those responsible for data protection, should provide oversight and guidance on the use of AI systems while respecting individuals' privacy rights. - Special considerations should be given to areas where AI systems impact essential private and public services and benefits, especially concerning accessibility and inclusivity. - The fundamental right to the protection of personal data is safeguarded under Union data protection laws, emphasizing the importance of these rights in the context of AI.
