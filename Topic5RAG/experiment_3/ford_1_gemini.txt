The correct spark plug gap for a Model T Ford depends on your ignition setup, but the "gold standard" used by most owners is 1/32 of an inch (approximately .031").

If you are looking for a quick answer, you can't go wrong with .030". However, here is the breakdown of why you might adjust it slightly:

1. The Standard "By the Book" Specs
Original Ford Specification: 1/32" (.03125"). This is the classic measurement found in the original Ford Service Manual.

The "Dime" Rule: In the old days, a common "field adjustment" was to use a thin dime as a feeler gauge. A modern dime is roughly .053", so this only works if the dime is significantly worn down! Stick to a real feeler gauge if you have one.

Pro Tip: The "Drip Loop"
When you gap your plugs (especially Champion X), ensure the side electrode is bent so that the tip points slightly downward toward the center. This creates a "drip loop" so that any oil that gets into the combustion chamber runs off the electrode rather than bridging the gap and fouling the plug.
