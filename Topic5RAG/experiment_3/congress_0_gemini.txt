On the week of January 13, 2026, U.S. Congressman Mike Flood (R-NE) delivered a speech on the House floor to honor David Black, the longtime Mayor of Papillion, Nebraska.

The remarks were made following Mayor Black’s announcement that he would not seek re-election after serving more than 22 years in various roles for the city, including over 17 years as Mayor. In his tribute, Mr. Flood highlighted several key points regarding the Mayor’s legacy:

Economic Stewardship: He credited Mayor Black with transforming Papillion into a "thriving economic hub," specifically noting his success in attracting major firms like Google and Facebook to expand in the city.

Public Service: He described the Mayor as a "paragon of public service" and emphasized the strong foundation he built through partnerships with the business community and professional city staff.

Community Impact: Mr. Flood stated that the Mayor would be "deeply missed" by the residents and expressed hope that Black would continue his commitment to public service in other capacities in the future.

Personal Note: He concluded by saying that Mayor Black had "more than earned" the opportunity to spend more time with his family after his final term concludes.
